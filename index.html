Correlation

- Linear relationship between 2 continuous variables

- Eg: BMI & SBP.

- One of the assumptions is there is no outlier (>3 Std. Deviation).

- Strong positive correlation = more than 0.5

- Strong negative correlation = less than -0.5

- Eg. -0.307 = weak negative correlation.

- Remember:

  • 0 = No correlation.
  • +1 = Perfect positive correlation.
  • -1 = Perfect negative correlation.

- Additional notes:

  • two-tailed test = Is the calculated value either below or above expectation?
  • one-tailed test = Is the calculated value below/above expectation?

- Eg. of one-tailed: We test a new drug, is it better than old drug?



Linear Regression

Simple Linear Regression

- Predict dependent variable, from independent variable. [P-D-F-I]

- Involve only one dependent & independent variable.

- Eg: Predict SBP (dependent), from BMI (independent).

- 6 assumptions:

  1. Continuous independent variable.
  2. Continuous dependent variable.
  3. Has linear relationship (do correlation test first).
  4. No autocorrelation. (bias due to non-random sampling).
  5. Homoscedasticity (all dots uniformly distributed around central line).
  6. No outliers (very high/very low value).
